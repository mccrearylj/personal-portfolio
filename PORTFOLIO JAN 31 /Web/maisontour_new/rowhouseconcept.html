Why the Row House?

The row house is a historic residential building type commonly found in American and European cities. These structures blend singlefamily dwellings into durable, vibrant multifamily neighborhoods that establish an effective density in the sustainable urban environment. The guiding principle of effective density suggests that low-rise buildings create an appropriate, natural scale to achieve both energy selfsufficiency and livable neighborhoods while assimilating easily across a multitude of established or developing urban fabrics.
