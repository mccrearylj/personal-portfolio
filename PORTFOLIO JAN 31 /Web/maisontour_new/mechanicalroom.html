Nuts 'N Bolts

The mechanical room resides behind the kitchen and under the stairs. This is where the balance of electrical equipment is housed.

The order of systems activation is based feedback loops in the system that tell it how quickly comfort conditions are being met as well as giving priority to solar based energy systems over systems that may require sourcing energy from the grid if the solar resources is poor or absent and the home's electrical storage system (either a battery bank or an electric vehicle) are depleted. To achieve comfort conditions in the home, several systems can work together or separately based on demand. The home's systems related to achieving comfort conditions include a custom, student designed and manufactured Integrated Solar AirtoAir collector and Air-to-Air Phase change Storage system (ISAAC and AAPS), an ERV, a customized descant wheel dehumidification system, a high efficiency watertoair heat pump, and a solar thermal system.

Maison Reciprocity uses a unique combination of novel and off-the-shelf technologies to achieve comfort conditions through its mechanical systems. All MEP systems in the home are located within the CHORD, one of Maison Reciprocity's three main building components. A student-created algorithm coupled with Arduino based software and hardware comprise the home's control system and it engages one or multiple systems in a specified order to meet occupant demands for various comfort conditions.
