Components

The home features two bathrooms, one servicing each level of the prototype. The bathrooms and kitchen use low flow fixtures and low flow, dual flush toilets which feature a space saving inwall tank design. These highly efficient building components minimize water use in the home without sacrificing style, elegance, and functionality.

Water efficiency

Solar thermal

The home's solar thermal system is coupled to both the domestic hot water system and the HVAC system (see the attached schematic diagram for additional information on system components and interaction).

The three collector solar thermal system is sized to provide nearly 100% of the home's predicted hot water demand and meet a significant portion of its annual heating requirements. The drainback solar thermal system, uses evacuated tube collectors as well as a variable speed, high efficiency pump and two 303 liter hot water storage tanks. One tank is used for domestic hot water and the other is used as an HVAC process heat storage tank. A three way valve switches between which tank is heated by the solar thermal collectors giving priority to heating domestic hot water. Heat from the process heat storage tank is used in a heat exchanger located directly in a modified air handler as well as for regenerating the descant wheel in the dehumidification system.
