Heating Mode

In heating mode, the home's algorithm seeks to first use energy from ISAAC and AAPS, then the solar thermal system, and finally the heat pump allowing the home to heat in the most cost effective manner possible without sacrificing comfort. The dehumidification system and ERV are used as necessary throughout both heating and cooling processes based on system sensors and feedback loops or passive conditioning strategies like night flushing. A Haiku ceiling fan is also employed to prevent stratified temperature conditions in the home's vaulted rooms.

While a typical home would use a standard heat pump, which is costly and inefficient, Maison Reciprocity sports three sources of heat: an air足to足air phase change system, or AAPS, a Process Heat Tank, which is essentially a hot water heater, and finally, as a last resort, a standard heat pump.
